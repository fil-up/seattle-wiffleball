West Coast Washout 12, The Dinger Squad 9  


 The West Coast Washout used nine first inning singles to build a 7-run lead which they would never relinquish. &#160;Pitching on his birthday, Washout starter Jason Matt &#160;bent but didn't break, mixing Dinger Squad walks, hits, and runs in with enough strikeouts and pop-outs to limit the Dingers to chipping away. &#160;The game had the feeling of slipping away from the Washout until a two-out double by Austin Cudworth plated Kevin Tsuchida and Andrew Winter in the fourth. &#160;Matt was fired up and in control in his last inning on the mound as he struck out the final two batters to preserve the 12-9 lead. 


  
West Coast Washout 3, The Dinger Squad 2  


 Andrew Winter and Aiden Welch were both lights out. &#160;Had they pitched in opposite game, we would have almost surely seen a split. &#160;As it was, the matchup produced some fantastic action. &#160;Winter struck out 13 batters, while Welch took down 7. &#160;The score was knotted at 0-0 going into the third until Austin Cudworth led off with a single. &#160;After an out, Winter helped his own cause by walking, before Daniel Rish doubled, bringing home Cudworth. &#160;Winter and Rish scored as the result of an errant peg attempt in what was the Dinger Squad's only mistake of the day. &#160;Trailing 3-0 in the final inning, the Dinger Squad showed great poise at the plate, loading the bases via two walks and a hit. &#160;Winter responded by striking out one batter, and inducing a pop-fly. &#160;The next batter he walked, prompting captain Daniel Rish to pull Winter and insert himself at pitcher, in a decision sure to be remembered as foolish if it didn't pay off. &#160;Winter had only walked 6 in the game, but 3 of those came in the sixth. &#160;When Cody Thomson walked on a 3-1 pitch to bring the tying run 45 feet from home, the Dinger Squad let out a collective bellow of triumph. &#160;It looked like their joy might increase as a visibly shaken Rish fell behind in the count 2-1 to Welch. &#160;However, with the game on the line, Rish was vindicated in his decision-making as Welch swung through a full count riser.

  Pitcher of the Week: Andrew Winter  


 Andrew led the West Coast Washout to a 3-2 victory in the first pitchers' duel in league history, striking out 13 while scattering 7 hits and 6 walks. &#160;Winter's repertoire includes a curveball which has yet to be adapted to the Wiffle game, but it was sharp enough to baffle Dinger Squad hitters. &#160;After struggling with control in his first two starts, Winter was sharp throughout. &#160;Winter was pulled one out before completing the game, and the bullpen let in the second run of the game, though it was charged to Winter. &#160;Winter flirted with the 70 mph speed limit throughout, several times skipping right past the flirting stage. 


  Honorable Mention: Matt Soucy (W 11-1 vs. Wiff Waff: 6IP, 3H, 4  BB, 6  K)  

 
 
 
 &#160; &#160;  
 

  Defensive Player of the Week: Aidan Welch  


 &#160;Aidan's big plays came in the first inning, as he robbed homeruns from the West Coast Washout's Daniel Rish in consecutive at-bats. &#160;Those two plays saved somewhere around 6 runs, as a grand slam and a 3-run homerun were converted to a single sac fly. &#160;Welch's defensive stand did not stop there. &#160;He continued to nab fly balls out of the sky in the first game of the series, and then fielded his position flawlessly from the mound in game 2.

 &#160;  

 
 

  Offensive Player of the Week:&#160;   Matt Soucy 


 The Bungalow Boys came crashing onto the scene with a bang, with Matt Soucy being a leading force at the plate. &#160;His day started off somewhat slowly, as he went 3-7 against Felix Reyes of Wiff Waff. &#160;His shortcomings were largely due in overagressiveness, and he popped out three times (a fourth was a sacrifice fly). &#160;In the second game, Matt came a home run short of the cycle, and piled up 8 RBIs while going 5-7. &#160;We salute Matt for his aggressive approach, which will pay long-term dividends if it can be refined and combined with patience.
